## ARTICLE II - DEFINITIONS

**"Act"** means the Pennsylvania Limited Liability Company Law of 1994, as amended.

**"Capital Contribution"** means any contribution of cash, property, services, or promissory obligations by a Member to the Company.

**"Distributable Cash"** means cash available for distribution after payment of operating expenses, debt service, and reasonable reserves.

**"Interest"** or "Membership Interest"** means a Member's entire ownership interest in the Company, including economic and governance rights.

**"Member"** means any person or entity holding a Membership Interest.

**"Percentage Interest"** means a Member's share of Company profits, losses, and distributions as set forth in Schedule A.

## ARTICLE IV - CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

### 4.1 Initial Capital Contributions
The initial Member has contributed or agreed to contribute the capital set forth in Section 3.1.

### 4.2 Additional Contributions
No Member shall be required to make additional Capital Contributions. Additional contributions may be made at the Member's discretion.

### 4.3 No Interest on Capital
No Member shall be entitled to interest on any Capital Contribution.

### 4.4 Return of Capital
No Member shall have the right to withdraw or receive a return of any Capital Contribution except as provided in this Agreement or upon dissolution.

### 4.5 Capital Accounts
The Company shall maintain a Capital Account for each Member in accordance with Treasury Regulation § 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv).

---

## ARTICLE V - ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

### 5.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses
Net profits and losses of the Company shall be allocated to the Members in proportion to their Percentage Interests.

### 5.2 Distributions
Distributions of Distributable Cash shall be made at such times and in such amounts as determined by the Member(s), in proportion to Percentage Interests. For so long as there is a single Member, that Member may make distributions at their sole discretion.

### 5.3 Tax Distributions
If the Company has taxable income allocated to Members, the Company shall endeavor to distribute sufficient cash to enable Members to pay their tax obligations arising from such allocation (a "Tax Distribution"), subject to the Company's cash availability and business needs.

### 5.4 Limitations on Distributions
No distribution shall be made if, after giving effect to the distribution:
- The Company would be unable to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of business; or
- The Company's total liabilities would exceed its total assets.

## ARTICLE VI - MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Member-Managed
The Company shall be member-managed. All management authority and decisions shall be vested in the Member(s).

### 6.2 Powers of Member
The Member shall have full and complete authority, power, and discretion to manage and control the business, affairs, and properties of the Company, including but not limited to:
- Opening and maintaining bank accounts
- Entering into contracts
- Hiring employees and contractors
- Acquiring, holding, and disposing of property
- Borrowing money and granting security interests
- Filing tax returns and making tax elections
- Any other actions necessary or appropriate to carry on the Company's business

### 6.3 Officers
The Member may appoint officers (such as President, Secretary, Treasurer) to carry out day-to-day operations. Officers serve at the pleasure of the Member(s) and have such authority as delegated.

### 6.4 Compensation
The Member shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Company, as determined by the Member in good faith.

### 6.5 Indemnification
The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member and any officers, employees, or agents from any claims, losses, or liabilities arising from their good faith conduct of Company business, to the fullest extent permitted by the Act.

---

## ARTICLE VII - ADMISSION OF NEW MEMBERS

### 7.1 Admission Requirements
Additional Members may be admitted to the Company only with the unanimous written consent of all existing Members and upon:
- Execution of a written agreement to be bound by this Operating Agreement (as amended);
- Payment of any required Capital Contribution; and
- Amendment of this Agreement to reflect updated Percentage Interests.

### 7.2 Terms of Admission
Upon admission of new Members, the existing Member(s) shall negotiate and document:
- Capital contribution requirements
- Percentage Interest to be granted
- Vesting schedule, if applicable
- Voting and governance rights
- Role and responsibilities
- Any restrictions on transfer

### 7.3 Amendment Upon Admission
Upon admission of any new Member, this Agreement shall be amended and restated as a Multi-Member Operating Agreement, incorporating appropriate provisions for:
- Voting thresholds for major decisions
- Dispute resolution mechanisms
- Rights of first refusal on transfers
- Buy-sell provisions
- Non-compete and non-solicitation terms, if applicable

### 7.4 Equity Incentive Plan (Reserved)
The Member may establish an equity incentive plan to grant Membership Interests, profits interests, or phantom equity to employees, contractors, or advisors. Such plan shall be documented separately and incorporated by reference.

---

## ARTICLE VIII - TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

### 8.1 Restrictions on Transfer
No Member may sell, assign, transfer, pledge, or otherwise dispose of any Membership Interest without the prior written consent of all other Members (or, in the case of a single Member, at the Member's sole discretion).

### 8.2 Permitted Transfers
Notwithstanding Section 8.1, a Member may transfer Membership Interests to:
- A revocable living trust for estate planning purposes, provided the Member is the trustee;
- An immediate family member, with consent of other Members;
- Another Member of the Company.

### 8.3 Effect of Unauthorized Transfer
Any attempted transfer in violation of this Article shall be null and void.

---

## ARTICLE IX - DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### 9.1 Events of Dissolution
The Company shall be dissolved upon the earliest of:
- The written consent of all Members;
- The sale of all or substantially all Company assets;
- Entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act;
- Any event making it unlawful for the Company to continue; or
- (In a single-member LLC) the death, incapacity, or bankruptcy of the sole Member, unless a successor is designated or a new Member is admitted within 90 days.

### 9.2 Winding Up
Upon dissolution, the Member(s) (or a liquidating trustee) shall wind up the Company's affairs, liquidate assets, pay creditors, and distribute remaining assets to Members in accordance with their Capital Account balances.

### 9.3 Certificate of Dissolution
Upon completion of winding up, the Company shall file a Certificate of Dissolution with the Pennsylvania Department of State.
