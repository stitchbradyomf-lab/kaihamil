# Groove Pal — Brand Guide

**A pal to whom you send your groove.**

---

## Elevator Pitch

Welcome to Groove Pal — a pen-pal style vinyl record sharing program and platform that promotes experiencing and sharing analog activities with others. The platform tracks the journey of a record and enables sharing a conversation about the listening experience, bringing people together over shared non-screen time experiences.

## Brand Essence

### What We Are
A vinyl sharing network built on trust, patience, and presence.

### What We're Not
- A marketplace (we don't sell records to each other)
- A collector community (we don't hoard or grade)
- A social network (we don't have feeds or likes)
- A subscription box (you don't receive, you participate)

### The Core Insight
Music gets better when it travels. Records gain meaning from the people who've loved them.

---

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

**Warm but not saccharine**
We're genuine without being performative. Think: a friend who really means it when they ask how you're doing.

**Patient but not passive**
We move slowly on purpose. That's different from moving slowly because we don't care.

**Trusting but not naive**
We believe in people. We also understand that not everyone will participate the way we hope. That's built into the system.

**Poetic but not pretentious**
We find meaning in small things. We don't lecture about it.

### Tone Examples

**Instead of:** "Join our community of music lovers!"
**Write:** "Become a pal."

**Instead of:** "Our platform enables seamless vinyl sharing."
**Write:** "Records travel. Stories grow."

**Instead of:** "Earn rewards for your engagement!"
**Write:** "Share more. Get more."

**Instead of:** "We're disrupting the way people experience music."
**Write:** "This is the way it used to feel."

## Language Patterns

### Words We Use
- Pal, pals (never "members" or "users")
- Groove (the music, the feeling, the connection)
- Journey (where a record travels)
- Story (what accumulates on a sleeve)
- Share, send, pass (active generosity)
- Listen (with intention)
- Trust (our currency)
- Patience (our tempo)
- Presence (our practice)

### Words We Avoid
- User, member, subscriber
- Platform, service, product
- Engagement, content, algorithm
- Disruption, innovation
- Community (overused; we say "network" or "circle")
- Exclusive, premium, VIP

### Phrases That Capture Us
- "A pal to whom you send your groove."
- "Music, mail, and meaning."
- "Trust is the feature, not a bug."
- "Every friction is a feature."
- "The record tells its own story."
- "The groove moves when you do."

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary**
- **Cream** (#FAF6F1) — Background, warmth, paper
- **Vinyl Black** (#1A1A1A) — Text, contrast, the record itself
- **Amber Gold** (#C9A227) — Accent, warmth, nostalgia

**Secondary**
- **Warm Brown** (#6B5344) — Secondary text, earthiness
- **Terracotta** (#B87A5E) — Highlights, energy
- **Soft White** (#FFFDF9) — Cards, brightness

### Typography

**Headlines:** Serif with character
- Cormorant Garamond, Freight Text, or similar
- Warm, slightly vintage, readable

**Body:** Clean sans-serif
- Inter, Söhne, or similar
- Modern, approachable, doesn't fight the content

**Accent:** Handwritten moments
- For personal notes, sticker designs
- Authentic imperfection

### Photography Style

**Subject Matter:**
- Record sleeves with stickers
- Turntables in lived-in spaces
- Hands holding vinyl
- Mailboxes and packages
- Maps and journeys

**Treatment:**
- Warm color grading (not cold or clinical)
- Natural light preferred
- Slightly desaturated (analog feel)
- Focus on texture and detail
- Real spaces, not studios

**Avoid:**
- Overly styled/curated shots
- Digital-looking imagery
- Stock photo aesthetics
- Anything that feels "brand"

### Iconography

**Style:** Simple line icons with warmth
- Rounded corners
- Single stroke weight
- Organic imperfection welcome

**Key Icons:**
- Record/vinyl disc
- QR code/sticker
- Envelope/mail
- Map pin/journey
- Heart/thanks
- Person/pal

## Physical Design

### Stickers

**Size:** 1" x 1" square with rounded corners

**Elements:**
- QR code (center)
- "groove pal" wordmark (small, bottom)
- Unique ID (tiny, bottom corner)

**Paper:** Matte finish, durable outdoor vinyl
**Feel:** Should age gracefully, not peel easily

### Mailers

**Style:** Rigid record mailer with custom sleeve

**Exterior:**
- "Handle with care — music inside"
- Groove Pal logo (subtle)
- Space for handwritten address

**Interior:**
- Welcome note pocket
- Instructions for first-timers

**Color:** Kraft/natural brown (recyclable)

### Packaging Insert

**Welcome Card:**
- "Welcome, Pal."
- Brief instructions (listen → attach → send)
- QR code to groovepal.com/start

## Brand Applications

### Website (groovepal.com)
- Warm, minimal, focused
- Large typography
- Record journey as hero visual
- Smooth, patient scroll behavior
- No pop-ups or aggressive CTAs

### Email
- Plain text aesthetic (even if HTML)
- Personal from-line: "Your Groove Pal"
- Subject lines that feel like notes, not marketing

### Social (if any)
- Instagram only (visual medium)
- Focus on journey stories, not promo
- User-generated content preferred
- Rare posting cadence (quality > frequency)

## Brand Manifesto

We believe music is meant to be shared, not hoarded.

We believe patience is a form of presence.

We believe trust can be built between strangers.

We believe stories travel better on vinyl.

In a world of infinite scroll and instant everything, we choose to be slow. It takes days for a record to arrive. It takes time to sit down and listen. It takes effort to write a note and mail it forward.

That's the point.

Every friction is a feature. Every delay is a gift. The wait makes the arrival meaningful. The physicality makes the connection real.

We're not building a platform. We're tending a network. We're not acquiring users. We're welcoming pals.

The groove moves when you do.

**A pal to whom you send your groove.**
