1. Data Product Owner

Script Template

Opening
"As a Data Product Owner at [type of organization], I had to [specific challenge]."

[Your specific story: shipping a vendor quality platform with 40+ stakeholders, all wanting different things. Endless alignment meetings, PowerPoints but no product.]

2. Technology Project Manager

🎥

Video Placeholder: 90 seconds

Record: "As a Technology Project Manager..."

Script Template

Opening
"As a Technology Project Manager, I had to migrate a legacy data pipeline to the cloud... with zero downtime, 99.5% accuracy, and a team that had never done infrastructure work."
The Challenge (30-45s)
[Technical risk was manageable. Human risk wasn't. Engineers terrified of breaking $40B in daily transactions. Three levels of approval. Progress at speed of fear.]
The Insight (15-20s)
[Stopped managing project, started architecting confidence. Shadow system running parallel. Technical achievement secondary. Psychological safety primary.]
The Framework (15-20s)
[Infrastructure-First: Build the container before pouring the liquid. Most projects fail not because work is hard, but because confidence isn't structured.]
Closing Question
"What are you trying to pour before you've built the container?"

3. Workflow Developer

🎥

Video Placeholder: 90 seconds

Record: "As a Workflow Developer..."

Script Template

Opening
"As a Workflow Developer, I had to automate vendor operations that had been done manually for 15 years... without the vendors realizing anything changed."
The Challenge (30-45s)
[Process held together by tribal knowledge. One person knew exceptions, another knew client rules. Documentation was folklore. Automating documented process would break everything.]
The Insight (15-20s)
[Started with observation, not automation. Watched for two weeks. Found 60% of 'process' was workarounds for upstream problems. Automating bad process makes bad output faster.]
The Framework (15-20s)
[Life Process Mapping: Understand actual flow before optimizing. Most skip this step. Build what people SAY they do instead of what they ACTUALLY do. The gap is where automation breaks.]
Closing Question
"Before you automate, are you mapping the real process or the assumed one?"

4. Quality Analyst

🎥

Video Placeholder: 90 seconds

Record: "As a Quality Analyst..."

Script Template

Opening
"As a Quality Analyst, I had to improve data accuracy from 92% to 99.5%... across 12 different data sources, each with their own definition of 'correct.'"
The Challenge (30-45s)
[Every source owner swore their data was clean. Cross-reference found 40% of 'quality issues' were integration mismatches - timestamps, units, null handling. Not quality control problem. Quality translation problem.]
The Insight (15-20s)
[Stopped measuring accuracy, started measuring alignment. Built 'common cause variation' dashboard showing where discrepancies came from. For first time, could see real problem.]
The Framework (15-20s)
[Common Cause vs Special Cause: Is your problem systemic or situational? Most quality efforts fail because they treat symptoms (special causes) when disease is structural (common causes).]
Closing Question
"Are you fixing the glitch or the system that produces glitches?"

5. Intelligence Analyst

🎥

Video Placeholder: 90 seconds

Record: "As an Intelligence Analyst..."

Script Template

Opening
"As an Intelligence Analyst, I had to deliver actionable insights to executives... from incomplete data, under time pressure, with high-stakes decisions hanging on my analysis."
The Challenge (30-45s)
[Data was ambiguous. Timeline impossible. Executives wanted certainty. Found myself either oversimplifying - confident answers I knew were incomplete - or overqualifying - burying insights in caveats. Neither served the decision.]
The Insight (15-20s)
[Started framing output as 'decision support' rather than 'analysis.' Instead of 'data shows,' said 'given what we know, range of reasonable outcomes is...' Gave them uncertainty, structured. They could make call with eyes open.]
The Framework (15-20s)
[Uncertainty Framework: Moving from 'what do I know' to 'what do I know about what I don't know.' Intelligence isn't about being right. It's about being calibrated. Decision-makers don't need certainty. They need clarity on uncertainty.]
Closing Question
"When you don't have perfect information, do you hide the gaps or illuminate them?"

6. People Leader

🎥

Video Placeholder: 90 seconds

Record: "As a People Leader..."

Script Template

Opening
"As a People Leader, I had to scale a team from 5 to 25 people... without losing the culture, burning out the veterans, or drowning myself in management overhead."
The Challenge (30-45s)
[Every new hire changed chemistry. Every process documented created rigidity. Every meeting added reduced output. Became a bottleneck - every decision flowed through me. Team growing, but effectiveness shrinking.]
The Insight (15-20s)
[Shifted from 'managing people' to 'designing systems that manage the work.' Built checklists instead of checking work. Created decision frameworks instead of resolving conflicts. Built dashboards instead of being source of truth.]
The Framework (15-20s)
[System//Self balance for leaders. Self work - coaching, vision, culture - requires that System work - process, structure, automation - is handled. Can't be present for people if drowning in operational noise.]
Closing Question
"Are you leading people or managing the work they do? The difference is burnout."

Recording Specifications

Visual Setup

  • Camera: Eye level, 6 feet away, torso-up
  • Background: Neutral office or soft home setting
  • Lighting: Window or softbox from front-left
  • Audio: Lapel mic or phone voice memo held close

Delivery Style

  • Tone: Conversational, not presentational
  • Pacing: Pause after challenge. Let it land.
  • Eye contact: Camera lens, not screen
  • Energy: Highest on insight/framework transition

Production

  • Raw footage: 3-5 minutes per script
  • Edit to: 90 seconds
  • Captions: Required (silent viewing)
  • Thumbnail: Face + "As a [Persona]..." text

Distribution

  • LinkedIn: Primary (professional credibility)
  • YouTube Shorts: Secondary (discovery)
  • Kai Hamil site: Embed in framework pages
  • Email: Include transcript for readers
