The Architecture of Emotional Response

How External Events Become Internal Experiences

⚡ Automatic Processing

Layers 1-4 operate below conscious awareness in milliseconds. You cannot directly control these—but you can influence what data enters them.

🧠 Cognitive Processing

Layers 5-6 involve conscious evaluation. You can learn to pause here and deliberately reappraise before emotion crystallizes.

🎯 Highest Leverage

Layer 7 (Mindset) is the "override filter" that skews ALL other processing. This is where intentional work yields the greatest returns.

EMOTIONAL EXPERIENCE

🍼 The Baby Test

A baby lacks Layer 3 schemas for "spilled milk = problem." Without interpretive context, no emotional charge exists. Emotions require meaning.

🔄 Information Reversal

When triggering information is corrected (false lottery win, mistaken death notice), emotion must recalibrate. Emotion follows information.

📊 Same Event, Different Outcome

Spilled milk → tears (fixed mindset) OR eager cleanup (growth mindset) OR shrug (no relevance). Processing determines response.

The Core Insight

Emotions are not triggered by events—they are constructed through processing.
The same external stimulus, passing through different filters (schemas, appraisals, mindsets), produces entirely different emotional experiences. What is constructed can be reconstructed.

Based on Lazarus Cognitive Appraisal Theory (1991), Stress Mindset Research (Crum et al., 2013-2017), and Emotion Regulation Science (Gross, Ochsner)
