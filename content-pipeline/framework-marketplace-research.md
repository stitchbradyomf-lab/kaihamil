## Successful Framework Formats & Patterns

**Date:** February 24, 2026

## Top-Performing Framework Formats

### 1. **The Step-by-Step Process**
**Examples:**
- IDEO's Design Thinking (5 phases)
- OODA Loop (Observe → Orient → Decide → Act)
- Eisenhower Matrix (4 quadrants)

**Pattern:**
- Clear numbered steps
- Visual diagram showing flow
- Each step has actionable prompts
- Summary checklist at end

**Why it works:** Reduces cognitive load, creates sense of progress

---

### 2. **The Diagnostic/Assessment Tool**
**Examples:**
- Myers-Briggs Type Indicator
- StrengthsFinder
- Enneagram
- DISC Assessment

**Pattern:**
- Series of questions
- Scoring mechanism
- Archetype/output based on answers
- Personalized recommendations

**Why it works:** People love learning about themselves; creates investment

---

### 3. **The Matrix/Grid**
**Examples:**
- BCG Matrix (Stars/Cash Cows/Question Marks/Dogs)
- Eisenhower Matrix (Urgent/Important)
- Johari Window (Known/Unknown to self/others)
- RACI Matrix

**Pattern:**
- 2×2 or larger grid
- Two axes representing key variables
- Clear zone definitions
- Action guidance per zone

**Why it works:** Visual, memorable, easy to place yourself/situation

---

### 4. **The Causal Loop/Systems Map**
**Examples:**
- Flywheel (Jim Collins)
- Vicious/Virtuous Cycles
- Stock-and-Flow diagrams
- ICE Scoring (Impact/Confidence/Ease)

**Pattern:**
- Shows relationships between variables
- Arrows indicating direction of influence
- Feedback loops highlighted
- Intervention points marked

**Why it works:** Reveals hidden structure, explains why simple fixes fail

---

### 5. **The Mental Model/Principle**
**Examples:**
- First Principles Thinking (Musk/Tesla)
- Inversion (Munger)
- Second-Order Thinking
- Hanlon's Razor

**Pattern:**
- Core principle stated clearly
- Examples of application
- Common pitfalls/traps
- When to use/when not to use

**Why it works:** Portable, applies across domains, becomes part of vocabulary

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with the visual** — People process diagrams faster than text
2. **Make it interactive** — Engagement = retention
3. **Provide instant utility** — Something they can use in 5 minutes
4. **Enable sharing** — Watermark with brand, make it quotable
5. **Create sequels** — One framework leads to another
