# 5 Filters for Deciding Under Uncertainty

## The Problem

You're facing a consequential decision. The information you need doesn't exist—or you can't access it—or you don't know which parts to trust.

Most people handle this poorly. They freeze (analysis paralysis) or rush (impulsive choices they'll regret).

Here's a framework for deciding when you can't know everything.

---

## Filter 1: Optionality

**Does this preserve future choices?**

High optionality: Learning skills, building networks, saving money.

Low optionality: Burning bridges, over-specializing in dying fields, isolating yourself.

The trap: Optionality feels like anxiety rather than freedom. Learn to tell the difference.

---

## Filter 2: Reversibility  

**How hard is this to undo?**

Easily reversible (new job, new city, side projects): Act fast. The cost of being wrong is low.

Nearly irreversible (children, major surgery, burned bridges): Slow down. Gather information. Sleep on it.

Most people spend equal energy on both. Don't.

---

## Filter 3: Information Value

**Will more data change my decision?**

Calculate: Cost of information × Probability it changes your mind × Magnitude of improvement.

If the answer is "probably not," decide now.

Exception: Irreversible decisions. For these, information has asymmetric value.

---

## Filter 4: The Premortem

Imagine it's one year later and this failed spectacularly. What went wrong?

This isn't pessimism—it's structured imagination. We tend toward optimism (planning fallacy). The premortem corrects this.

Then implement cheap preventions or build warning sign detectors.

---

## Filter 5: 10-10-10

How will I feel about this in:
- 10 minutes (emotional reaction—usually noise)
- 10 months (practical consequences)  
- 10 years (identity impact)

Most bad decisions overweight 10-minute feelings and underweight 10-year consequences.

---

## The Meta-Decision

Match your decision process to the stakes:

- **Low stakes, reversible:** Gut feeling. Decide quickly.
- **Medium stakes:** Use the 5 filters. Spend an hour.
- **High stakes, irreversible:** Full analysis. Consult experts. Sleep on it.

Don't over-optimize trivial decisions. Save your energy for what matters.

---

## Summary

Uncertainty is the default state of important decisions.

Don't chase certainty you can't have. Use filters that work with it:

1. Preserve optionality
2. Respect reversibility
3. Value information correctly  
4. Premortem failures
5. Use 10-10-10

Your future self is watching. Decide accordingly.
