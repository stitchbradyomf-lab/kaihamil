## Body (300 characters)
1. Optionality — preserve future choices
2. Reversibility — act fast if undoable  
3. Information value — don't research what won't change your mind
4. Premortem — imagine failure before it happens
5. 10-10-10 — don't let 10-minute feelings drive 10-year consequences

Decision paralysis?

Ask 3 questions:
• Can I undo this if wrong?
• Will more research actually change my mind?
• How will I feel in 10 years?

90% of decisions don't need more analysis. They need appropriate speed.

## Thread Version (5 posts)

Post 1/5: Most people handle uncertainty poorly. They freeze (analysis paralysis) or rush (impulsive choices). There's a better way. 5 filters for deciding under uncertainty:

Post 2/5: Filter 1: Optionality. Does this preserve future choices? High optionality = learning, networks, savings. Low = burning bridges, over-specializing in dying fields.

Post 3/5: Filter 2: Reversibility. Easily undone? Act fast. Nearly irreversible (kids, surgery, burned bridges)? Slow down. Spend 20% energy on reversible, 80% on permanent.

Post 4/5: Filter 3: Information value. Will more data change your decision? If no, decide now. Exception: irreversible decisions warrant more research.

Post 5/5: Filters 4-5: Premortem (imagine failure before it happens) + 10-10-10 (don't let 10-minute feelings drive 10-year consequences). Full framework: [link]
