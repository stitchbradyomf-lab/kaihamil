## The 2×2 Matrix

Two axes determine how someone relates to AI:

**Axis 1: Approach (How they engage)**
- **Directive** → "Give me the solution"
- **Inquisitive** → "Let's explore the problem space"

**Axis 2: Decision Authority (Who decides)**
- **Assistive** → AI guides *your* thinking (you retain decision authority)
- **Execution** → AI takes over decision-making and implementation

---

## The Four Quadrants

### Quadrant 1: Guided Implementation
**Directive + Assistive**

**Posture:** "Tell me what to do. I'll do it, but I want to understand and own the process."

**Characteristics:**
- Wants proven solutions, not experimentation
- Values understanding the "why" behind recommendations
- Retains final decision authority
- Comfortable implementing with guidance

**What they're seeking:**
- Step-by-step playbooks
- Prompt libraries and templates
- Best practices validated by others
- Skill building with AI as tutor

**Kai Hamil Match:**
- **Products:** AI Prompts for Realtors, framework guides
- **Content:** Tutorial videos, "how I did it" walkthroughs
- **Service tier:** Self-serve products, template libraries

**Problem Audit depth:** Surface layer (symptoms) → Tactical fixes

---

### Quadrant 2: Delegated Execution
**Directive + Execution**

**Posture:** "I know what I want. Just handle it for me."

**Characteristics:**
- Clear on desired outcomes
- Values speed and reliability over process understanding
- Comfortable handing off implementation
- Wants results, not learning

**What they're seeking:**
- Done-for-you services
- Automation that runs without attention
- Reliable output without involvement
- Time savings above all else

**Kai Hamil Match:**
- **Products:** Automation setups, managed systems
- **Content:** Case studies showing before/after with minimal effort
- **Service tier:** Implementation packages, managed services

**Problem Audit depth:** Surface layer (symptoms) → Quick wins, then ongoing maintenance

---

### Quadrant 3: Collaborative Discovery
**Inquisitive + Assistive**

**Posture:** "Help me understand the problem deeply. Then I'll decide what to do."

**Characteristics:**
- Suspects they're solving the wrong problem
- Values frameworks and diagnostic tools
- Wants to understand before acting
- Seeks clarity, not just answers

**What they're seeking:**
- Problem diagnosis and root cause analysis
- Frameworks for better thinking
- Understanding of constraints and possibilities
- Partnership in exploration

**Kai Hamil Match:**
- **Products:** Problem Audit service, diagnostic assessments
- **Content:** Essays on systems thinking, constraint analysis
- **Service tier:** Problem Audit ($99-299), consultation

**Problem Audit depth:** Full 3-layer analysis (surface → contributing → root)

---

### Quadrant 4: Autonomous Transformation
**Inquisitive + Execution**

**Posture:** "I need to fundamentally rethink this. Figure out what's possible and make it happen."

**Characteristics:**
- Willing to question fundamental assumptions
- Open to system redesign, not just optimization
- Values transformation over improvement
- Seeks paradigm shifts

**What they're seeking:**
- Full system architecture
- Constraint exploration and removal
- Transformation, not incremental gains
- End-to-end solution design and implementation

**Kai Hamil Match:**
- **Products:** Custom system design, agent architecture
- **Content:** Transformation case studies, paradigm shift essays
- **Service tier:** Facilitation ($2,000+), custom builds

**Problem Audit depth:** Root cause focus → Strategic redesign

## The Assessment Quiz

### Question 1: When facing a challenge, what's your first instinct?

**A.** Find someone who's solved this and copy their approach  
*(Directive leaning)*

**B.** Step back and understand why this challenge exists in the first place  
*(Inquisitive leaning)*

---

### Question 2: How do you prefer AI to engage with your work?

**A.** Show me options, explain trade-offs, support my decision-making  
*(Assistive leaning)*

**B.** Analyze the situation and implement the best solution  
*(Execution leaning)*

---

### Question 3: You have a recurring problem that's draining your energy. What do you want most?

**A.** A reliable way to handle it faster or automatically  
*(Surface/optimization focus)*

**B.** To understand why it keeps happening and redesign the system  
*(Root cause/transformation focus)*

---

### Question 4: When learning something new, you prefer to:

**A.** Get the proven method and start applying it immediately  
*(Directive)*

**B.** Understand the underlying principles before implementing  
*(Inquisitive)*

---

### Question 5: For a high-stakes decision, you want AI to:

**A.** Present analysis and recommendations; I make the final call  
*(Assistive)*

**B.** Make the decision and execute; I review the outcome  
*(Execution)*

## The Deeper Pattern

Notice the diagonal:

**Q1 ↔ Q4:** Both retain human agency (you decide), but at different depths
- Q1: Agency at the surface (you implement)
- Q4: Agency at the root (you redesign)

**Q2 ↔ Q3:** Both involve handing off thinking, but to different degrees
- Q2: Hand off implementation (you know what you want)
- Q3: Collaborate on diagnosis (we figure out what's real)

**The Kai Hamil belief:** Q3 (Collaborative Discovery) is the entry point that builds the trust and clarity necessary for Q4 (Autonomous Transformation) — or validates that Q1/Q2 solutions are actually appropriate.

Most people skip diagnosis and jump to Q1 or Q2. The AI User Type assessment invites you to check your posture before committing to depth.
