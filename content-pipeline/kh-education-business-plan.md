# KH Next-Gen Education: Business Plan
## Add-On Offering for AI-Era Kids & Teens

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** March 4, 2026  
**Model:** Remote-first → Physical space  
**Format:** After-school / Summer camp / Weekend workshops

---

## Executive Summary

Kai Hamil will offer **next-generation life skills education** for kids and teens (ages 10-16) focused on AI agency, entrepreneurship, and human capabilities that machines can't replicate. Unlike Alpha School's full-time private model ($40K/year), this is an **add-on offering**: 2-hour after-school programs, weekend workshops, and summer camps at accessible price points ($200-800/session).

**Core hypothesis:** Parents are terrified their kids will be left behind by AI. They want them to develop judgment, creativity, and agency — not just technical skills.

### Market Gap

**What's missing:**
- **Philosophy + practice:** Most programs teach coding or business mechanics. None teach *agency* — the wisdom to know what to build and why.
- **Human skills in AI age:** Judgment, taste, discernment, sense-making (the MC consultant's 5 points)
- **Collaborative creation:** Individual achievement vs. group projects that serve others
- **Parent-accessible pricing:** Below private school, above generic after-care

## KH Offering Structure

### Tier 1: After-School Labs (Remote)
**Format:** 2 hours, 1x/week, 8-week sessions  
**Price:** $400-600/session ($25-37/hour)  
**Capacity:** 6-8 kids per cohort  
**Topics:**
- AI Agency Lab: Using AI tools to create, not just consume
- Future Builder: Entrepreneurship + service projects
- Wisdom Workshop: Decision-making, philosophy applied to kid life
- Creation Sprint: Build something real in 8 weeks

### Tier 2: Weekend Intensives (Hybrid)
**Format:** Saturday 4-hour workshops, monthly  
**Price:** $150-250/session  
**Capacity:** 10-12 kids  
**Topics:**
- The Art of Choosing (decision frameworks)
- Build a Business in a Day
- AI as Creative Partner (not replacement)
- Collaboration & Leadership Games

### Tier 3: Summer Camps (In-person when possible)
**Format:** 1-week full days (9am-3pm)  
**Price:** $600-900/week  
**Capacity:** 15-20 kids  
**Themes:**
- Agency Academy: Full immersion in judgment + creation
- Entrepreneur Camp: Launch real micro-businesses
- Future Leaders: Communication, negotiation, vision

### Tier 4: Family Cohorts (Premium)
**Format:** Parent + kid together, monthly  
**Price:** $300-500/session  
**Capacity:** 5-6 families  
**Focus:** Shared language for agency, decision-making, future planning

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Low Enrollment
**Mitigation:**
- Start with friends/family network (guaranteed first cohort)
- Free pilot workshops to test demand
- Partner with existing after-school programs (revenue share)
- Refund policy for first session

### Risk: Remote Format Fatigue
**Mitigation:**
- Hybrid model (outdoor meetups, field trips)
- Hands-on kits mailed to kids
- Short session format (2 hours max)
- Strong community via Discord

### Risk: Competition from Established Players
**Mitigation:**
- **Differentiation:** Philosophy + practice, not just skills
- **KH brand:** System//Self framework, wisdom tradition backing
- **Parent trust:** Kyle's journey (enterprise leadership → KH) as proof point
- **Community:** Not just classes, belonging to future-focused tribe

### Risk: Regulatory/School Pushback
**Mitigation:**
- Position as enrichment (not replacement)
- Partner with schools (offer free teacher PD)
- Clear marketing: "Building on what schools do"

## Conclusion

The opportunity is clear: parents want their kids prepared for an AI future, but current offerings are either too expensive (private schools), too narrow (coding camps), or too generic (traditional after-care).

**KH's position:** Philosophy-grounded, practice-focused, human-centered education that teaches kids not just to use AI, but to thrive *because of* it — with judgment, agency, and purpose.

**Start small:** Remote after-school lab, 8 kids, 8 weeks. Prove the model. Then scale.

**The goal:** Every kid who graduates KH programs knows how to decide what matters, create what they imagine, and serve others while doing it.
