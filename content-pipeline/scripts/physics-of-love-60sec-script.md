# 60-Second "Physics of Love" Video Script
## For Valentine's Week Teaser

---

**[HOOK - 0:00-0:08]**

"In the movie Interstellar, a scientist makes a claim that sounds like poetry: 'Love is the one thing we're capable of perceiving that transcends dimensions of time and space.'"

**[PAUSE - Let it land]**

---

**[THE CHALLENGE - 0:08-0:18]**

"Hard scientists cringe at this. Romantics weep. But here's what's strange—the more you examine the research across neuroscience, longevity studies, and cross-cultural wisdom... the more that 'poetic' claim starts to look like an understatement."

---

**[THE EVIDENCE - 0:18-0:40]**

"When anthropologist Helen Fisher put people 'madly in love' into fMRI machines, she found something remarkable: romantic love activates the same brain regions as cocaine addiction. Dopamine floods the system. Rational decision-making goes partially offline."

"But this isn't a bug. It's a feature. Love, neurologically speaking, is a motivation system—not just an emotion, but a drive. It sits alongside hunger and thirst as one of the most powerful systems humans possess."

---

**[THE PAYOFF - 0:40-0:52]**

"The Harvard Grant Study followed people for 75 years. The director was asked what he'd learned. His answer: 'Happiness is love. Full stop.' The single strongest predictor of health and happiness at age 80 was the quality of relationships at age 50."

---

**[CALL TO ACTION - 0:52-1:00]**

"Not wealth. Not fame. Love. Full essay at kaihamil.com—link in bio."

---

## DELIVERY NOTES

**Tone:** Curious, authoritative but warm, slightly conspiratorial ("here's what they don't tell you")

**Pacing:** 
- Hook: Slow, deliberate
- Challenge: Slightly faster, building intrigue  
- Evidence: Measured, let the science land
- Payoff: Slow down for the Harvard quote
- CTA: Quick, casual

**Visual suggestions:**
- Open with Interstellar clip or still (fair use)
- fMRI brain scan imagery during science section
- Harvard campus/archival imagery during Grant Study
- Simple text overlay: "Happiness is love. Full stop."
- End card with URL

**Total runtime:** ~58 seconds (leaves room for natural pauses)
