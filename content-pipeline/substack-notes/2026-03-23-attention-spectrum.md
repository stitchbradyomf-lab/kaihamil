# The Attention Spectrum

*Substack Note — March 23, 2026*

---

I opened the top of my backpack and first took out my Fujifilm X100 VI mirrorless digital camera. A photographer always needs proper tools.

As I brought out my iPad and pondered whether to work or watch something, the moving light of the sunset over the gymnastics buildings in the warehouse struck a tone and needed to be captured.

I then realized that photography is equal parts consuming and producing. You are both choosing where to look and then making something original that represents your selective focus in that moment.

I realized immediately that on the spectrum of consuming versus producing, there were several more layers beneath. I defined photography as "capture" — a term that implies taking something and making it yours. I stuck it right in the middle of the consuming and producing chart.

Then I thought: what would the extreme ends of this spectrum be? What are the poles?

To the far left, I landed on **awareness** — almost unintentional consumption with little to no degree of further interpretation.

To the far right, I landed on **epiphany** — there's probably a better term than that, but I'm thinking some form of entirely original thought that makes everything else change beneath it.

I filled in the rest, extending capture to the right with **derivative** — a term I really just mean to be "based on something else." Everything here to the right can be considered new content. Even every photograph is a completely unique point in time and perspective.

On the left side, we have the distinction of **passive** and **active** consumption — describing the amount of focused energy it takes you to do the consuming. Reading a textbook and taking notes in the margins versus having the game on in your living room while you're cooking dinner. Two very different levels of focused attention.

**Art**, and **invention**, and **innovation**, each find their places on the right side of the spectrum.

I now have a map, a frame in which to describe activities and use of your time and focus. Your time and attention. The two resources over which you have full control.

How you spend your time, and where you direct your attention.

This framework becomes the plot — or at least one of the plots — to consider decision-making and finding equilibrium within your life.

---

**The Spectrum:**

`General Awareness` → `Passive` → `Active` → **Capture** → `Derivative` → `Art` → `Invention` → `Epiphany`

**CONSUMING ←——————————————————————→ PRODUCING**
