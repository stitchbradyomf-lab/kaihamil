## Experience Categories (6)

### 1. UNCERTAINTY & CHANGE 🌊
**The Experience:** Ground shifting beneath you, the uncontrollable, impermanence

**Traditions to Explore:**
- **Stoicism:** Amor fati (love of fate), dichotomy of control
- **Buddhism:** Anicca (impermanence), non-attachment
- **Taoism:** Wu wei (effortless action), flowing with change
- **Heraclitus:** "You cannot step twice into the same river"
- **Modern:** Nassim Taleb's antifragility, complexity theory

**System Connections:**
- Common Cause Variation (statistical thinking)
- 5 Filters (decision-making under uncertainty)
- Control Chart Evaluator (is this normal variation?)

**Key Insight:** Uncertainty isn't the enemy — resistance to uncertainty is.

---

### 2. LOVE & RELATIONSHIPS 💫
**The Experience:** Human connection in all its forms

**Already Exists:** The Physics of Love (Greek system)

**Additional Traditions:**
- **Confucianism:** Five relationships, filial piety, social harmony
- **Christianity:** Agape (unconditional love), caritas
- **Buddhism:** Metta (loving-kindness), compassion practice
- **Modern:** Attachment theory, love languages
- **Indigenous:** Community as extended family, collective care

**System Connections:**
- Emotional Response Process (navigating feelings)
- 5 Filters (choosing relationships wisely)

**Key Insight:** Love isn't one thing — it's a landscape we navigate poorly when we treat it as monolithic.

---

### 3. PURPOSE & WORK 🧭
**The Experience:** Finding meaning in what you do, the question of "why"

**Traditions to Explore:**
- **Aristotle:** Eudaimonia (flourishing), teleology
- **Japanese:** Ikigai (reason for being)
- **Protestant Work Ethic:** Calling, diligence, worldly asceticism
- **Marx:** Alienation, meaningful labor vs. survival
- **Bhagavad Gita:** Duty (dharma) without attachment to results
- **Modern:** Flow states, passion vs. purpose debate

**System Connections:**
- Life Portfolio Evaluation (allocation of time)
- Problem Audit (why does work feel empty?)
- Cause & Effect (redesigning your relationship to work)

**Key Insight:** Purpose isn't found — it's cultivated through action aligned with values.

---

### 4. SUFFERING & RESILIENCE ⚡
**The Experience:** When life breaks you, the inevitable pain of existence

**Traditions to Explore:**
- **Buddhism:** Four Noble Truths, dukkha (suffering), path to cessation
- **Book of Job:** The righteous sufferer, divine mystery
- **Stoicism:** Premeditatio malorum (preparing for hardship)
- **Nietzsche:** "What doesn't kill me makes me stronger," eternal return
- **Viktor Frankl:** Logotherapy, meaning in suffering
- **Modern:** Post-traumatic growth, resilience research

**System Connections:**
- Emotional Response Process (processing pain)
- Control Chart Evaluator (is this suffering normal variation or special cause?)

**Key Insight:** Suffering is inevitable; staying stuck in suffering is optional.

---

### 5. MORTALITY & TIME 🌙
**The Experience:** Confronting finitude, the scarcity of time, death awareness

**Traditions to Explore:**
- **Stoicism:** Memento mori (remember you will die), carpe diem
- **Tibetan Buddhism:** Bardo (intermediate state), death preparation
- **Epicureanism:** Death is nothing to us (when we exist, death doesn't)
- **Existentialism:** Authenticity through death awareness (Heidegger)
- **Mexican Day of the Dead:** Celebration of mortality, ancestor connection
- **Modern:** Terror management theory, longevity science

**System Connections:**
- 5 Filters (finite time forces prioritization)
- Life Portfolio Evaluation (time allocation = life allocation)

**Key Insight:** Mortality isn't a bug — it's the feature that gives life meaning.

---

### 6. AMBITION & DESIRE 🔥
**The Experience:** Wanting more, achievement, the tension between contentment and growth

**Traditions to Explore:**
- **Bhagavad Gita:** Duty without attachment to fruits of action
- **Daoism:** Natural ambition vs. forced striving, simplicity
- **Buddhism:** Tanha (craving) as root of suffering, right effort
- **Aristotle:** Magnanimity, proper pride, balanced ambition
- **Protestant/Puritan:** Calling, success as sign of grace
- **Modern:** Achievement culture, burnout, enoughness

**System Connections:**
- 5 Filters (is this ambition worth pursuing?)
- Cause & Effect (what system creates this desire?)

**Key Insight:** Ambition isn't bad — but unexamined ambition becomes a trap.
