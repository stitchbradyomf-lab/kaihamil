# KH Course Series: Agency by Type
## Personalized Pathways for the 4 AI User Types

**Framework:** Double Diamond (Explorer/Analyst/Designer/Builder) + The 5Cs  
**Goal:** Each type develops agency through their specific growth edge

---

## The Strategy

**The Quiz as Compass:**
- Take the AI User Type Quiz → Discover your type
- Each type has natural strengths and predictable blind spots
- Course targets the blind spot without suppressing the strength

**The Arc:**
1. **Type Discovery** — Understand your natural mode
2. **Blind Spot Awareness** — See what you miss
3. **Complementary Skills** — Develop what doesn't come naturally
4. **Integration** — Become "T-shaped" (depth in type + breadth across)

---

## Course 1: The Explorer's Path
**Subtitle:** *From Scattered to Strategic*

**The Explorer:**
- Strength: Seeing possibilities, asking questions, divergent thinking
- Blind Spot: Follow-through, depth, commitment paralysis
- Risk: "Analysis paralysis" or perpetual novelty-seeking

### The 5Cs for Explorers

**CHARACTER: Perseverance**
- The virtue of staying with one thing
- Practices: 30-day commitments, "depth over breadth" challenges
- Wisdom: Confucian "xue" (learning) requires sustained effort

**CRITICAL THINKING: Convergent Thinking**
- When to stop exploring and decide
- Frameworks: The 5 Filters, satisficing vs. maximizing
- Practice: "Good enough" decision protocol

**COLLABORATION: Deep Partnerships**
- Moving from many shallow connections to few deep ones
- Finding "Analyst" partners who help you focus
- Practice: Sustained mentorship relationships

**CREATIVITY: Constraint as Catalyst**
- Creating *within* limits rather than against them
- Practice: One-medium projects (write only poetry for a month)
- Wisdom: Crawford's "stubbornness of reality"

**CHOICE: Commitment**
- The courage to choose and close doors
- Practice: "Hell yes or no" + "commitment contracts"
- Anti-pattern: FOMO as avoidance of depth

### 8-Week Curriculum

**Week 1: Understanding Your Explorer Nature**
- The gift: Seeing what others miss
- The trap: Never diving deep
- Exercise: Map your "exploration history" — what did you abandon? Why?

**Week 2: The Philosophy of Depth**
- Stoic: "No person is free who is not master of themselves"
- Buddhist: The scattered mind vs. concentrated mind
- Exercise: Single-tasking challenge (one project, one week)

**Week 3: Convergent Thinking Tools**
- The 5 Filters applied to your life
- Satisficing: "Good enough" as optimal
- Exercise: Make one "irreversible" decision

**Week 4: The Art of Constraint**
- Why limits create (not restrict)
- Crawford: Craft requires submission to material limits
- Exercise: Create with artificial constraints (time, materials, format)

**Week 5: Deep Collaboration**
- Finding your "complementary type" (Analyst)
- Sustained relationship practice
- Exercise: 30-day accountability partnership

**Week 6: The Courage to Commit**
- Nietzsche: Amor fati — loving your choices
- Practice: Public commitment + follow-through
- Exercise: Launch something small, finish it

**Week 7: Integration — The Strategic Explorer**
- When to explore vs. when to exploit
- Building "exploration sprints" into your life
- Exercise: Design your personal "exploration budget"

**Week 8: Stewardship**
- Using your explorer gifts for others
- Becoming a "scout" for your community
- Final project: Guide others through unexplored territory

## Course 2: The Analyst's Path
**Subtitle:** *From Analysis to Action*

**The Analyst:**
- Strength: Precision, rigor, thoroughness
- Blind Spot: Action bias, shipping, "good enough"
- Risk: Perfectionism, paralysis by analysis, never launching

### The 5Cs for Analysts

**CHARACTER: Courage**
- The virtue of acting with incomplete information
- Practices: Deliberate imperfection, shipping before ready
- Wisdom: "The perfect is the enemy of the good"

**CRITICAL THINKING: Bayesian Updating**
- Start with priors, update with evidence
- Not: "Wait until certain" 
- But: "Act, observe, adjust"

**COLLABORATION: Builder Partnerships**
- Finding "Builder" partners who execute
- Delegating implementation
- Practice: Letting go of control

**CREATIVITY: Iteration**
- Creating through doing, not planning
- The "prototype mindset"
- Practice: Rapid iteration cycles

**CHOICE: Bias for Action**
- When 70% information is enough
- Practice: Decision velocity exercises
- Anti-pattern: Research as procrastination

### 8-Week Curriculum

**Week 1: Understanding Your Analyst Nature**
- The gift: Seeing clearly what others miss
- The trap: Waiting for perfect information
- Exercise: Audit your "infinite research loops"

**Week 2: The Philosophy of Action**
- Existentialist: "Existence precedes essence" — you become through doing
- Pragmatist: Truth is what works
- Exercise: Act before you feel ready (small scale)

**Week 3: The 80% Rule**
- When is good enough truly enough?
- Frameworks: Pareto principle, diminishing returns
- Exercise: Ship at 80% (journal, project, decision)

**Week 4: Iteration Over Perfection**
- The prototype mindset
- Learning through doing vs. learning before doing
- Exercise: Create → Ship → Iterate (one-week cycle)

**Week 5: Builder Collaboration**
- Finding partners who execute
- Delegating without micromanaging
- Exercise: Partner with a Builder on a small project

**Week 6: The Courage to Be Wrong**
- Reframing failure as data
- Practice: Public mistakes + recovery
- Exercise: Ship something you're unsure about

**Week 7: Integration — The Pragmatic Analyst**
- When to analyze vs. when to act
- Building "decision deadlines" into your process
- Exercise: Design your personal "action triggers"

**Week 8: Stewardship**
- Using your analytical gifts to guide others
- Helping Explorers focus, Builders plan
- Final project: Create a decision framework for others

## Course 3: The Designer's Path
**Subtitle:** *From Vision to Reality*

**The Designer:**
- Strength: Synthesis, aesthetics, user-centered thinking
- Blind Spot: Technical constraints, execution, "just building it"
- Risk: Beautiful concepts that never ship

### The 5Cs for Designers

**CHARACTER: Grit**
- The virtue of pushing through implementation hell
- Practices: Staying with the "boring" parts
- Wisdom: "The last 10% takes 90% of the effort"

**CRITICAL THINKING: Constraint Analysis**
- Engineering mindset: What's actually possible?
- Not: "Wouldn't it be cool if..."
- But: "Given these constraints, what's best?"

**COLLABORATION: Technical Partnerships**
- Working with Builders who can execute your vision
- Speaking the language of constraints
- Practice: Constraint negotiation

**CREATIVITY: Making Under Constraints**
- Beauty *through* limitations, not despite them
- Practice: Elegant solutions to hard problems
- Crawford: Mastery through submission to material

**CHOICE: Shipping**
- The decision to release imperfect work
- Practice: "Done is better than perfect"
- Anti-pattern: Eternal refinement

### 8-Week Curriculum

**Week 1: Understanding Your Designer Nature**
- The gift: Seeing what could be
- The trap: Beautiful concepts, zero execution
- Exercise: Audit your "design graveyard"

**Week 2: The Philosophy of Making**
- Crawford: "The satisfactions of manifesting oneself concretely"
- Value of the craftsperson
- Exercise: Make something physical with your hands

**Week 3: Constraint as Design Partner**
- Not obstacle, but opportunity
- Case studies: Great design from severe constraints
- Exercise: Design within artificial constraints

**Week 4: The Last 10%**
- Why execution is where design happens
- Pushing through "implementation hell"
- Exercise: Finish something you started (even if imperfect)

**Week 5: Builder Collaboration**
- Finding technical partners
- Your vision + their constraints = reality
- Exercise: Co-design with a Builder

**Week 6: Shipping Imperfection**
- The courage to release
- Feedback as gift, not judgment
- Exercise: Ship a "beta" version publicly

**Week 7: Integration — The Pragmatic Designer**
- When to push for perfection vs. ship
- Building "shipping deadlines" into your process
- Exercise: Design your personal "definition of done"

**Week 8: Stewardship**
- Teaching others to see possibilities
- Helping Analysts imagine, Builders envision
- Final project: Design something that gets built

## Course 4: The Builder's Path
**Subtitle:** *From Execution to Vision*

**The Builder:**
- Strength: Execution, reliability, getting things done
- Blind Spot: Strategic thinking, "why are we building this?", seeing alternatives
- Risk: Building the wrong thing beautifully

### The 5Cs for Builders

**CHARACTER: Reflection**
- The virtue of pausing to ask "why"
- Practices: Strategic thinking, long-term visioning
- Wisdom: "Efficiency is doing things right; effectiveness is doing the right things"

**CRITICAL THINKING: Systems Thinking**
- Seeing the bigger picture
- Not: "How do I build this?"
- But: "Should this be built? What are the effects?"

**COLLABORATION: Explorer/Designer Partnerships**
- Finding partners who see what you can't
- Receiving direction without feeling micromanaged
- Practice: Collaborative visioning

**CREATIVITY: Problem Reframing**
- Stepping back to see different approaches
- Not: "How do I solve this?"
- But: "Is this the right problem?"

**CHOICE: Strategic Direction**
- Choosing which mountains to climb
- Practice: Values-based project selection
- Anti-pattern: Busyness as avoidance of purpose

### 8-Week Curriculum

**Week 1: Understanding Your Builder Nature**
- The gift: Making things real
- The trap: Building without purpose
- Exercise: Audit your "built things" — which mattered? Why?

**Week 2: The Philosophy of Purpose**
- Aristotelian telos: Everything has an end/purpose
- Existentialist: You create the purpose
- Exercise: Define the "why" behind your current project

**Week 3: Systems Thinking**
- Seeing your work in context
- Second-order effects, unintended consequences
- Exercise: Map the system around your current project

**Week 4: The Art of Reflection**
- Building in pause points
- Strategic review practices
- Exercise: Weekly "strategy sessions" for your life

**Week 5: Explorer/Designer Collaboration**
- Finding partners who see possibilities
- Openness to direction and vision
- Exercise: Execute someone else's vision faithfully

**Week 6: Reframing Problems**
- Stepping back before diving in
- Alternative approaches to the same goal
- Exercise: Solve one problem three different ways

**Week 7: Integration — The Strategic Builder**
- When to execute vs. when to question
- Building "reflection rituals" into your process
- Exercise: Design your personal "purpose check"

**Week 8: Stewardship**
- Teaching others to execute
- Helping Explorers/Analysts/Designers make things real
- Final project: Build something that serves a larger purpose

## The Full Journey: Type Development

### Stage 1: Own Your Type (4 weeks)
- Deep understanding of your natural mode
- Appreciation for your gifts
- Recognition of your blind spots

### Stage 2: Develop the Complement (8 weeks)
- The course above tailored to your type
- Working with your growth edge
- Building complementary skills

### Stage 3: Cross-Training (8 weeks)
- Learn the adjacent type's skills
- Explorers → Analysts: Focus and rigor
- Analysts → Designers: Synthesis and vision
- Designers → Builders: Execution and grit
- Builders → Explorers: Possibility and curiosity

### Stage 4: Integration (4 weeks)
- Become "T-shaped" — depth in one, breadth across
- Lead teams with all four types
- Final project: Something that requires all four modes

## Assessment: Measuring Type Development

### Pre-Course
- AI User Type Quiz (confirm type)
- Self-assessment: "Where do I get stuck?"
- 360 feedback: "What do others see as my blind spot?"

### During Course
- Weekly reflection: "When did I use my type? When did I stretch?"
- Project milestones: Evidence of growth edge development
- Peer feedback: "What did you notice about my approach?"

### Post-Course
- Retake AI User Type Quiz (has balance shifted?)
- Portfolio review: Projects that demonstrate growth
- Self-assessment: "What can I do now that I couldn't before?"
