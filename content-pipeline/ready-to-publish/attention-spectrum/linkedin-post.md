# LinkedIn Post: The Spectrum of Attention

---

We talk about "consumers" and "creators" like they're different species.

But that binary misses where the real transformation happens.

I've been working on a framework that maps the full spectrum between pure consumption and pure production:

**Awareness** → **Passive** → **Active** → **Curation** → **Capture** → **Derivative** → **Innovation** → **Art** → **Epiphany**

The key insight: Position 5 — Capture — is the threshold.

Below it, you're working with existing content.
Above it, you're making new content.

Photography lives exactly at this hinge point. You're not inventing the scene — but you're deciding what to preserve, how to frame it, what matters. That choice is already creative.

The spectrum helps answer two questions:

1. **Where is your attention actually going?** (Not where you think it goes — where it actually goes)

2. **Is that where you want it?**

Because at the end of the day, you control exactly two resources: your time and your attention. Everything else flows from how you allocate them across this spectrum.

The goal isn't to "create more." It's to move deliberately.
