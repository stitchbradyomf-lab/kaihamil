# Twitter/X Thread: The Spectrum of Attention

---

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
The sunset demanded to be seen.

As I reached for my camera, I realized: photography isn't consumption OR creation.

It's both.

That led me to map the entire spectrum of how we spend attention. 🧵

---

**Tweet 2**
We talk about "consuming content" vs "creating content" like they're binary.

They're not.

There's a spectrum with 9 positions:

CONSUMING ←―――――――――→ PRODUCING

---

**Tweet 3**
The consuming side:

1. Awareness — background noise
2. Passive — TV on while cooking
3. Active — reading with a pen
4. Curation — choosing what deserves your gaze

---

**Tweet 4**
The threshold:

5. **Capture** — photography lives here

Taking something from the world and making it yours.

Still working with what exists, but creating something unique.

---

**Tweet 5**
The producing side:

6. Derivative — remixes, commentary
7. Innovation — new methods
8. Art — expression beyond utility
9. Epiphany — original thought that rearranges everything

---

**Tweet 6**
You control two resources:

• Time — how you spend it
• Attention — where you direct it

Every hour falls somewhere on this spectrum.

---

**Tweet 7**
The question isn't whether consuming is bad or producing is good.

The question: Does your allocation match your intention?

Most of us default left. Everything right of "capture" requires making something new.
