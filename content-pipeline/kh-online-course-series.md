## Stage 1: The Toolkit — Decision-Making for Complex Times

**Subtitle:** *Five lenses. Any problem. Clearer thinking.*

**Duration:** 4 weeks  
**Time Commitment:** 2-3 hours/week  
**Target:** Ages 11-16  
**Prerequisite:** None

### The Promise
Your child will learn five philosophical frameworks for making better decisions — about school, friends, technology use, and life direction. They'll stop defaulting to "what everyone does" and start asking "what would a wise person do?"

### Week 1: The Stoic Lens — What You Control
**Core Question:** What is in my power, and what isn't?

**Lessons:**
- Video: The Stoic dichotomy of control (10 min)
- Exercise: The "worry audit" — categorize your stresses
- Practice: One day of focusing only on what you control
- Worksheet: Personal "control circle" diagram

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI creates things. You choose which things matter."
- Exercise: When AI gives you 10 options, how do you choose?

### Week 2: The Buddhist Lens — The Second Arrow
**Core Question:** What is the pain, and what is my story about the pain?

**Lessons:**
- Video: The Buddha's parable of the second arrow (10 min)
- Exercise: Separate events from reactions
- Practice: Noticing the "second arrow" for one day
- Worksheet: Reframe one current struggle

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI fails. How you respond is up to you."
- Exercise: Design a "graceful failure" protocol

### Week 3: The Virtue Lens — Who Do I Want to Become?
**Core Question:** What would a flourishing person do?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Aristotle's virtue ethics (12 min)
- Exercise: Identify your "ideal self" in three areas
- Practice: One virtue-focused action per day
- Worksheet: Character compass design

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI can do your homework. Should it? Who do you become if it does?"
- Exercise: The integrity audit — what would you do even if AI could do it?

### Week 4: The Utility Lens — What Creates the Most Good?
**Core Question:** What maximizes positive impact?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Utilitarian thinking (10 min)
- Exercise: Map second and third-order consequences
- Practice: One decision analyzed for ripple effects
- Worksheet: Impact assessment template

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI scales everything. Make sure you're scaling good."
- Exercise: Design an AI-powered project that helps others

### Stage 1 Deliverable
**Personal Decision Toolkit:** A laminated card (digital or physical) with the five questions to ask before any big decision.

### Stage 1 Completion
- Badge: "Decision-Maker"
- Invitation to Stage 2 with early-bird pricing
- Parent report: Your child's decision-making style

## Stage 2: The Practice — Living Wisdom Daily

**Subtitle:** *From knowing to doing. The habits of agency.*

**Duration:** 6 weeks  
**Time Commitment:** 3-4 hours/week  
**Target:** Ages 12-16  
**Prerequisite:** Stage 1 or application

### The Promise
Your child moves from philosophical concepts to daily practices. They'll build specific habits around attention, purpose, difficulty, and presence — tested in the real world, not just discussed.

### Week 1: The Attention Practice
**Core Question:** Where does my attention go, and why?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Heidegger on authenticity + Buddhist mindfulness (15 min)
- Exercise: Attention audit — track where your focus goes for 3 days
- Practice: Design a "presence protocol" for device use
- Challenge: One hour of deep work daily (no AI assistance)

**Live Session (Week 1):** Cohort discussion — what did you notice about your attention?

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI is designed to capture attention. Take it back."
- Exercise: Build an "intentional AI use" plan

### Week 2: The Purpose Practice
**Core Question:** What am I doing, and why?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Ikigai + Existentialist meaning-making (15 min)
- Exercise: Ikigai mapping for your actual life
- Practice: Connect daily tasks to larger purpose
- Challenge: Design a "meaningful week"

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI can optimize your tasks. Only you can optimize your purpose."
- Exercise: The purpose hierarchy — which tasks serve your why?

### Week 3: The Difficulty Practice
**Core Question:** When should I choose the hard way?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Nietzsche on challenge + Stoic voluntary discomfort (15 min)
- Exercise: Identify your "right difficulties"
- Practice: Choose the hard way for one thing this week
- Challenge: Complete a project without AI shortcuts

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI removes friction. Some friction builds strength."
- Exercise: The difficulty decision tree — when to automate, when to manual

### Week 4: The Connection Practice
**Core Question:** How do I show up fully for others?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Greek philia + Attachment theory (15 min)
- Exercise: Relationship mapping — who matters, and how?
- Practice: Three "turn toward" moments daily
- Challenge: Deep conversation without devices

**Live Session (Week 4):** Partner exercise — practice authentic communication

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI can simulate conversation. It cannot simulate care."
- Exercise: Design a "no-AI" communication protocol for important relationships

### Week 5: The Letting Go Practice
**Core Question:** What can I release?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Buddhist non-attachment + Taoist wu wei (15 min)
- Exercise: Identify attachments (outcomes, identities, possessions)
- Practice: Release one attachment this week
- Challenge: Impermanence meditation + reflection

**AI Application:**
- Lesson: "AI changes constantly. Your peace cannot depend on any one tool."
- Exercise: The tool-agnostic mindset — skills that transfer

### Week 6: Integration Week
**Core Question:** What is my daily wisdom practice?

**Lessons:**
- Video: Synthesis — building a personal system (20 min)
- Exercise: Design your morning/evening wisdom routine
- Practice: One week of full integration
- Challenge: Teach one concept to a family member

### Stage 2 Deliverable
**Personal Wisdom System:** A documented daily practice (morning routine, decision protocols, evening reflection) tailored to your life.

### Stage 2 Completion
- Badge: "Practitioner"
- Invitation to Stage 3
- Parent report: Your child's emerging philosophy

## Stage 3: The Philosophy — Creating Your Compass

**Subtitle:** *Your personal operating system for the AI age.*

**Duration:** 8 weeks  
**Time Commitment:** 4-5 hours/week  
**Target:** Ages 13-16  
**Prerequisite:** Stage 2 or intensive application

### The Promise
Your child synthesizes everything into a coherent personal philosophy — a compass for navigating the complex decisions of adolescence and the AI age. They'll produce a manifesto and present it to the cohort.

### Week 1: The Foundation — What Matters Most?
**Core Work:** Values clarification + hierarchy

**Lessons:**
- Video: Values across wisdom traditions (20 min)
- Exercise: The five values exercise — identify your non-negotiables
- Practice: Test values against recent decisions
- Work: Begin personal philosophy document

### Week 2: The Self — Who Am I Becoming?
**Core Work:** Identity beyond achievement

**Lessons:**
- Video: Existentialist authenticity + Hindu dharma (20 min)
- Exercise: The "eulogy virtues" — who do you want to be remembered as?
- Practice: One week of "identity-aligned" actions
- Work: Draft "My Identity" section

### Week 3: The Work — What Is Mine to Do?
**Core Work:** Purpose + contribution

**Lessons:**
- Video: Aristotelian telos + Japanese ikigai (20 min)
- Exercise: The convergence map — passion, skill, need, reward
- Practice: Prototype a contribution project
- Work: Draft "My Work" section

### Week 4: The Relationships — Who Is My Community?
**Core Work:** Connection + service

**Lessons:**
- Video: Greek agape + Care ethics (20 min)
- Exercise: Relationship portfolio — audit and invest
- Practice: Design a service project
- Work: Draft "My Relationships" section

### Week 5: The Difficulties — What Am I Willing to Endure?
**Core Work:** Choosing your challenges

**Lessons:**
- Video: Nietzschean amor fati + Stoic premeditation (20 min)
- Exercise: The hardship hierarchy — what is worth suffering for?
- Practice: One chosen difficulty
- Work: Draft "My Difficulties" section

### Week 6: The Technology — How Do I Relate to AI?
**Core Work:** Human + machine integration

**Lessons:**
- Video: Philosophies of technology + KH System//Self (25 min)
- Exercise: Design your "AI use philosophy"
- Practice: One week of intentional AI use
- Work: Draft "My Technology" section

**Live Workshop (Week 6):** Cohort discussion — sharing AI philosophies

### Week 7: Synthesis — Pulling It Together
**Core Work:** Manifesto writing

**Lessons:**
- Video: The art of personal philosophy (20 min)
- Exercise: Full document review and refinement
- Practice: Daily recitation of key principles
- Work: Complete personal philosophy manifesto

### Week 8: Presentation — Sharing Your Compass
**Core Work:** Showcase + community

**Lessons:**
- Video: Teaching as learning (15 min)
- Exercise: Prepare 10-minute presentation
- **Final Live Workshop:** Cohort presentations to parents + peers
- Work: Final manifesto polish + share

### Stage 3 Deliverable
**The Personal Philosophy Manifesto:** A 10-20 page document (written, video, or mixed media) that articulates:
- Core values and why they matter
- Identity and who I'm becoming
- Purpose and contribution
- Relationship philosophy
- Approach to difficulty
- Technology integration principles
- Daily practices to embody all of the above

### Stage 3 Completion
- Badge: "Philosopher"
- Certificate of completion
- Invitation to KH advanced programs / mentorship
- Parent showcase event
- Optional: Manifesto featured on KH website (with permission)
