## Summary

Longtermism - the philosophical view that positively influencing the long-term future is a key moral priority - continues to generate intense debate at the intersection of ethics, AI safety, and Silicon Valley influence.

## What is Longtermism?

Three core tenets:
1. **Future people matter** just as much as those alive today
2. **The future could be vast** - potentially billions of years of human civilization
3. **We can reliably influence** how the future goes

## The Controversy

### Criticism
- **Power Concentration**: Critics argue longtermism risks "locking in authoritarianism" as a means to solve future problems
- **Neglect of Present**: When accounting for future populations, present suffering (global poverty) becomes "a rounding error"
- **Silicon Valley Ties**: The movement's intimacy with tech billionaires raises concerns about "arbitrary power"

### Defense
- Future people have no voice in present decisions that affect them
- Existential risks (AI, pandemics, climate) could foreclose trillions of future lives
- Moral impartiality suggests we shouldn't discount future generations

## Current Context (April 2026)

A recent 34th Street Magazine article (April 2026) explores how effective altruism and AI alignment have become intertwined, with Peter Singer's drowning child analogy extended to future generations not yet born.

## Implications

- **AI Safety**: Longtermism underpins much of the existential risk framing around AGI
- **Resource Allocation**: Debates over whether to fund malaria bed nets vs. AI alignment research
- **Political Philosophy**: Questions about who gets to decide humanity's long-term trajectory
