## Summary

Record Store Day 2026 (April 18) arrives as the vinyl revival reaches new heights, with the global market projected to hit $4.18 billion. Over 350 exclusive vinyl releases are dropping for the 19th annual celebration of independent record stores.

## Key Details

- **Market Size**: Global vinyl market projected at $4.18 billion for 2026
- **US Milestone**: Vinyl sales surpassed $1 billion in the United States in 2025 (RIAA data)
- **RSD 2026**: 350+ exclusive releases, happening Saturday, April 18, 2026
- **Notable Releases**: 
  - The Cure, Nas, Miles Davis, Pavement exclusives
  - doPE (Chuck D + John Densmore) debut album "No Country for Old Men" (limited to 2,000 copies)
  - New Rolling Stones single "Rough And Twisted" under moniker The Cockroaches

## The Analog Revival

Vinyl's resurgence isn't just nostalgia - it's become a cultural statement in the streaming age. Independent record stores have become community hubs, and physical media ownership is experiencing a renaissance across formats.

## Implications

- **Physical Media Persistence**: Despite digital dominance, analog formats create tangible value
- **Indie Retail Resilience**: Record Store Day has become a critical revenue event for independent shops
- **Collector Economy**: Limited releases driving scarcity-based demand
