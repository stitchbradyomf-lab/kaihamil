## 📍 DAY-BY-DAY ITINERARY

### **DAY 1 - Monday, November 2: Arrival & Reykjavik Exploration**
- **Morning:** Arrive Keflavik Airport, pick up rental car
- **Late Morning:** Check into hotel (early check-in may be available)
- **Afternoon:** Explore downtown Reykjavik
  - Hallgrímskirkja Church (iconic landmark)
  - Sun Voyager sculpture
  - Harpa Concert Hall (free to walk around)
- **Evening:** Early dinner at family-friendly spot (Bæjarins Beztu hot dogs are a must!)
- **Late Evening:** First Northern Lights tour (if forecast is good)

**Meals:** Lunch at airport or downtown, Dinner in Reykjavik

---

### **DAY 2 - Tuesday, November 3: Golden Circle Day Trip**
- **Morning (9am departure):** Drive Golden Circle (~3 hours total driving)
  - **Thingvellir National Park** - Walk between tectonic plates (kids love this!)
  - **Geysir Geothermal Area** - Watch Strokkur erupt every 5-10 minutes
  - **Gullfoss Waterfall** - Massive two-tiered waterfall
- **Afternoon:** Return to Reykjavik for warm indoor time
- **Evening:** Dinner + Northern Lights tour or self-drive hunt

**Driving:** ~250 km round trip, ~3 hours
**Tips:** Start early to maximize daylight, bring snacks

---

### **DAY 3 - Wednesday, November 4: South Coast Adventure**
- **Morning (8:30am):** Drive along South Coast
  - **Seljalandsfoss Waterfall** - Walk behind the waterfall!
  - **Skógafoss Waterfall** - 60m high, often has rainbows
- **Lunch:** Vik village or Skógar
- **Afternoon:** 
  - **Reynisfjara Black Sand Beach** - Dramatic basalt columns (stay away from water!)
  - **Optional:** Sólheimajökull Glacier (easy walk to view)
- **Evening:** Return to Reykjavik, warm dinner, early night

**Driving:** ~300 km round trip, ~4 hours
**Tips:** Pack warm, waterproof clothing; this is a long day

---

### **DAY 4 - Thursday, November 5: Blue Lagoon & Relaxation**
- **Morning:** Sleep in, leisurely breakfast
- **Late Morning:** Drive to Blue Lagoon (45 min from Reykjavik)
  - **Note:** Kids under 2 not allowed; ages 2-13 require parental supervision
  - Book entry time in advance (required)
  - Silica mud masks included
- **Afternoon:** Soak and relax
- **Late Afternoon:** Return to Reykjavik
- **Evening:** Family dinner, Northern Lights hunt (self-drive or tour)

**Alternative:** If Blue Lagoon is too expensive/crowded, try:
- **Sky Lagoon** (Reykjavik) - infinity edge with ocean views
- **Laugarvatn Fontana** - less crowded, more local feel

**Cost:** Blue Lagoon ~$65-90/adult, ~$35-45/child (ages 2-13)

---

### **DAY 5 - Friday, November 6: Reykjavik Museums & Souvenirs**

  - **Perlan Museum** - Interactive exhibits, simulated ice cave, 360° observation deck
  - **Whales of Iceland Exhibition** - Life-size whale models
  - **Saga Museum** - History with costume photo ops (kids love this)
- **Afternoon:** Souvenir shopping on Laugavegur street
- **Evening:** Farewell dinner, final Northern Lights attempt

---

### **DAY 6 - Saturday, November 7: Departure**
- **Morning:** Breakfast, check out
- **Late Morning:** Drive to airport (45 min), return rental car
- **Afternoon:** Flight home

## 🚗 TRANSPORTATION

### Rental Car vs Tours

**RECOMMENDATION: Rental Car + Select Tours**

**Rental Car:**
- **Pros:** Flexibility, can chase northern lights independently, easier with kids
- **Cons:** Winter driving conditions, parking in Reykjavik
- **Cost:** ~$60-100/day for SUV/4WD (recommended in November)
- **Book:** Cars.co.is, Blue Car Rental, or major chains

**Northern Lights Tours:**
- **Pros:** Expert guides know best spots, free retry if no lights seen
- **Cons:** Fixed schedule, late nights with kids can be tough
- **Cost:** ~$60-100/person
- **Recommended Operators:** Arctic Adventures, Reykjavik Excursions

**Golden Circle & South Coast:**
- Can self-drive OR join small group tours
- With kids, having your own car offers more flexibility for breaks
