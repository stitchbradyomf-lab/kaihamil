```
The #1 organizational mistake I see small business owners make:

Using their email inbox as their task manager, filing system, and to-do list.

Your inbox is where OTHER people put their priorities. Not where you should store yours.

Here's what to do instead:

→ Inbox = processing station, not storage
→ Move actionable items to a real task system
→ File reference emails immediately
→ Archive or delete everything else

Goal: Get to inbox zero daily (yes, really).

The mental clarity is worth the 15 minutes it takes.
```

## Discovery Call Script Outline

### Opening (2 min)
- Thanks for taking the time
- Brief intro: what I do in one sentence
- "My goal for this call is to understand your current situation and see if I can actually help. If I can't, I'll tell you."

### Current State (10 min)
- "Walk me through: when a new client comes in, what happens? Where does stuff go?"
- "What tools/apps are you currently using?"
- "When's the last time you couldn't find something important?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your organizational systems right now?"

### Pain Points (5 min)
- "What's the biggest organizational headache in your business?"
- "How much time do you think you lose to organizational chaos per week?"
- "What have you tried before? Why didn't it work?"

### Vision (3 min)
- "If you had a magic wand and could snap your fingers—what would 'organized' look like for you?"
- "What would change in your business/life if you had that?"

### Bridge (5 min)
- Brief explanation of what I do
- How it would apply to their specific situation
- "Here's what I'm thinking..."

### Next Steps (5 min)
- If fit: Explain packages, recommend one, discuss timeline
- If not fit: Honestly explain why, offer alternative resources
- Either way: "Any questions for me?"

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Summary

**Primary:** Solo service providers
- Realtors (especially top producers who've grown faster than their systems)
- Business coaches and consultants  
- Marketing/creative agency owners (1-5 people)
- Financial advisors
- Lawyers (small practice)

**Revenue:** $100K-$1M annually (enough to invest, not so big they have ops staff)

**Psychographics:**
- Self-aware about their organizational weakness
- Values their time highly
- Has tried apps/tools before (and failed)
- Some anxiety about "balls dropping"
- Open to spending money on solutions (not DIY-obsessed)

**Red flags:**
- Wants someone to DO the work (that's a VA)
- Can't commit 10 min/day to habits
- Business is too chaotic to systematize (pre-product-market-fit)
- Extreme budget sensitivity
