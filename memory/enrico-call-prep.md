# Enrico Call Prep - March 4, 3 PM
**Context:** ML Strategy CTO. Kyle seeking vision/guidance on evolving vendor ops as AI/ML becomes ubiquitous - *not* supporting Enrico's efforts, but aligning manual work with advancing automation.

## Opening / Frame the Conversation
1. "I wanted to bring you up to speed on my new role leading vendor operations. As AI/ML becomes more ubiquitous, I'm thinking about how vendor ops needs to evolve - and I'd value your perspective on where things are heading."

2. "Right now my team manages a lot of manual vendor processes. I'm trying to understand how those should adapt as automation advances. What's your view on where ML is actually ready to take over vs where humans still need to be in the loop?"

## Strategic Direction Questions
3. "From your vantage point, what should a modern vendor ops function look like 2-3 years out? Are there capabilities we should be building now?"

4. "What patterns are you seeing in how vendor relationships *should* change as AI tooling becomes standard? Are there contract structures, governance models, or oversight approaches that make more sense?"

5. "Where do you see the biggest gaps between what vendors promise (AI/ML-enabled) and what actually delivers? I want to make sure we're evaluating vendors through the right lens."

## Practical Application
6. "If you were designing vendor ops from scratch today - knowing what's coming in ML/AI - what would you do differently from traditional approaches?"

7. "Are there specific AI/ML use cases you've seen actually work well in vendor management contexts? (Not hype, real results)"

8. "What signals should I look for to know when a vendor's 'AI capabilities' are real vs just marketing?"

## Org/People Readiness
9. "As we start leaning into more automated vendor processes, what skills or roles do you think become more critical on my team?"

10. "From an ML strategy perspective, where does vendor data become valuable? Should I be thinking about our vendor data as a training asset or just operational overhead?"

## Closing
11. "Given where ML is heading, what's one thing I should stop doing, one thing I should start doing, and one thing I should keep doing in vendor ops?"

12. "Would you be open to periodic check-ins as we pilot new approaches? Your read on what's actually working vs what's just buzz would save us a lot of trial and error."
