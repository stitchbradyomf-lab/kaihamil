## Morning Reflection: Scientific Method as Meta-Process

**Context:** Carl Sagan clip on science vs. religion - the question of whether science can determine what created the universe.

**Key Insight:**
- Science is the **Method** (System)
- God/Creator is the **Cause** (potential answer)
- Science paves paths, disproves possibilities, but doesn't necessarily explain origins

**Role Mapping:**
- **Scientists** → Explore/Experiment (Explorer type) - asking questions, forming hypotheses
- **Operations** → Execute/Enable (Builder type) - greasing wheels, gathering materials
- **Decision makers** → Allocate/Commit (Analyst type) - conclusions presented for action

**The Scientific Method as THE process:**
1. Ask a question
2. Form a hypothesis
3. Establish procedure
4. Measure results
5. Analyze data
6. Render conclusion

"To do any less is to miss a crucial step."

**Connection to KH:** This maps directly to the Cause & Effect framework - science as *process* (System) to discover *cause* (underlying reality). The distinction between "what is" (observable/testable) and "why there is anything at all" (metaphysical) is the System/Self boundary.
